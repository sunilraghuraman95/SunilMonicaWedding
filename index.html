Events

8th July

Sangeet 6.30pm - 11pm

Sangeet is a pre-wedding celebration in Indian weddings that involves music, dance, and singing. It is a joyful occasion for the families of the bride and groom to come together and celebrate the upcoming union. One of the highlights of the Sangeet ceremony is the couple's dance, which is a special performance by the bride and groom. This is typically a choreographed dance that is performed to a romantic song and is meant to showcase the couple's love and chemistry. It is a time for families to come together, celebrate, and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Sangeet will be preceeded by the engagement ceremony, known as Nischayathartham, where the families of the bride and groom formally announce their intention to get married.

9th July

Wedding 9am - 11am

The wedding ceremony is a very traditional event involving the tying of the sacred thread, or the Mangalsutra, around the bride's neck, which symbolizes the union of the couple. One of the most important aspects of Tamil Brahmin weddings is the emphasis on the role of the family and the community. The wedding is seen as a union not just of two individuals, but of two families, and there are many rituals and ceremonies that reflect this.

Reception 6:30pm - 11pm

The reception is typically a post-wedding celebration that is held to honor the newlywed couple. The reception typically includes a sit-down dinner, music and dance performances. The reception is also an opportunity for the couple to exchange gifts and take photos with their guests. It provides an opportunity for the couple to celebrate their union with their loved ones. It is a joyous and festive occasion that is filled with food, music, dance, and love.
